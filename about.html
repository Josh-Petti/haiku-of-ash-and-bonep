About Haiku of Ash and Bone

A serialized war novel by J.M. Mikael

Haiku of Ash and Bone is a long-form serialized war novel set in a brutal, Sengoku-era style world. It follows civilians, soldiers, and people caught between clans, superstition, and survival. This is not a heroic fantasy. Villages burn. People break. Power wins first. Mercy comes later, if at all.

Where to Begin

This story is released as a free web novel and all chapters are published online, in order, as an ongoing series instead of all at once like a traditional book. Web serials build tension by letting readers follow events as they happen, week by week.

Release Schedule

New chapters go live on Tuesdays.

Major updates, new art, music drops, or structural changes to the site are announced through the mailing list. If you want to be notified the moment something changes, join the list. Otherwise, check the Archive.

Content Warning

This is a violent story. It includes:

  • war crimes against civilians
  • psychological pressure, fear, and coercion
  • blood, injury, and death on-page
  • exploitation and power imbalance
  • supernatural horror elements rooted in folklore and belief

It does not romanticize any of that.

Why This Exists

Stories like this rarely arrive neat from a publisher. Serialized fiction lets writers tell the story they actually want to tell, build an audience directly, and release in real time without waiting for permission. Haiku of Ash and Bone is being published independently, chapter by chapter, as a record of a world collapsing and the people trying to live through it.

Who Is Behind It

All writing on this site is by J.M. Mikael.

How to Follow

  • Read the chapters in order from the Table of Contents.
  • Check Tuesdays for new releases.
  • Join the mailing list if you want alerts the second a chapter or new art drops.
  • Come back. The world changes.

That is it. No corporate feed. No algorithm. Just you and the story.

How to Support

If this work resonates with you, consider supporting future chapters through Ko-fi or Patreon. One hundred percent of contributions go back into funding the roadmap for Haiku of Ash and Bone.
